Nonlinear Quadcopter Control in MATLAB/Simulink

Built a physics-based nonlinear 6-DOF quadcopter digital twin in MATLAB/Simulink for closed-loop control design. The model included discrete-time simulation, stochastic disturbances, sensor noise, and structured parametric uncertainty.

I evaluated trajectory-tracking controllers using time-varying linearization, including finite-horizon LQG, model predictive control, feed-forward control, and robust H∞ methods.

Vehicle Dynamics Simulation and Control in MATLAB/Simulink

Developed a medium-fidelity vehicle dynamics simulation using the Magic Formula tire model to study torque input, longitudinal velocity, and behavior under changing friction conditions.

Implemented algebraic derivative estimators to estimate acceleration from speed data and applied a model-free control law derived from a linearized model. Added torque input limiters to reflect realistic system constraints.

Neonatal Video Laryngoscope

Led a six-person team in the design of a neonatal video laryngoscope with attention to human factors, functionality, and a cost cap of $3000.

Performed finite element analysis in Abaqus on tissue-contacting components, designed a blade latching mechanism with a safety factor of 4.7 under bending loads, and created an electronic circuit schematic in KiCad.

Enhancing Heat Dissipation in Power Electronics

Conducted REU research on advanced cooling design for power electronics using integrated microfluidic systems. Built a 1:1 computational model, ran heat dissipation simulations in Abaqus, and compared fin-layout designs for improved thermal performance.

The resulting heat sink concepts improved efficiency by approximately 74% to 94% under high-power operating conditions.

Quarter-Car Active Suspension Control with LQR and Tube MPC

This project compares open-loop, LQR, and Robust model predictive control (MPC) for a quarter-car active suspension model in MATLAB. The objective was to improve ride comfort and disturbance rejection while respecting actuator and state constraints.

The quarter-car model used four states representing tire deflection, unsprung mass velocity, suspension deflection, and sprung mass velocity. The active suspension force acted as the control input, and the road profile entered as a disturbance. I evaluated controller performance on two road profiles: a bump/hole disturbance and a realistic IRI 737b road input.

I first simulated the open-loop system to identify the most disturbance-sensitive states. Then I tuned a discrete-time LQR controller and used that weighting structure to build a constrained finite-horizon MPC controller. The final implementation used tube MPC to improve robustness and feasibility under bounded disturbances.

I also compared controller behavior with and without road-preview information. With preview enabled, the controller could anticipate upcoming road features within the prediction horizon and reduce transient peaks more effectively.

Tools: MATLAB, YALMIP, quadratic programming, state-space modeling, LQR, tube MPC

Key ideas: active suspension, constrained control, preview control, robust feasibility, ride comfort vs. control effort tradeoff
